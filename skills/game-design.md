# Game Design Skill

## Purpose

Use this guide when making game design decisions for **Yelkenli Yaşam Tycoon**. The game should consistently feel like a progression-based sailing-life tycoon, not a collection of disconnected mechanics.

## Core Design Direction

- The main loop should support growth from a small sailing-life business into a larger media, route, and sponsorship operation.
- Player motivation should come from visible progress, better options, stronger rewards, and meaningful long-term goals.
- The sailing-life fantasy should stay present in the game loop through routes, sea decisions, upgrades, content, and world-tour progression.
- Daily goals should provide short-term direction and retention support without replacing the core loop.
- Upgrades should feel like strategic investments that unlock stronger future play rather than instant win buttons.
- Captain XP and level should reinforce long-term growth and give the player a clear sense of development.
- World tour progress should remain the big meta progression target that ties the whole experience together.

## Design Priorities

- Favor progression, clarity, and replayable decision-making.
- Create meaningful choices with advantage/cost balance.
- Keep early game active and satisfying.
- Support medium-term goals through upgrades, followers, and sponsorships.
- Support long-term goals through captain level and world-tour advancement.

## What To Avoid

- Do not add random features that do not strengthen the main game loop.
- Do not introduce mechanics that distract from routes, content, upgrades, sea choices, progression, or economy flow.
- Do not add complexity just to make the game feel bigger.
- Do not replace existing working systems unless there is a clear design reason.

## Working Style

- Prefer small, testable game design improvements.
- When changing progression behavior, think about early game, mid-game, and long-term growth.
- When proposing a feature, explain how it improves motivation, pacing, or meaningful choice.
- Preserve completed systems unless a change clearly improves the design.
